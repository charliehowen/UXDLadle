Usability Test Plan

What Hypothesis Are We Testing?

Explain the hypothesis that your formal study would allow you to test.

We decided to conduct a usability test on the graph of summary information displayed on the home page. As a team, we have been discussing the multiple ways to display a user’s budget status — pie graph, bar graph, exploded pie graph, and multiple pie graphs. We are unsure which form will most effectively displays the relevant information. Existing budget apps often use a pie graph, but we feel that this may not be the best representation of the budget. This homepage is a central part of our app that the user will look at the most. Choosing the wrong form of graph can prevent users from having an easy understanding of their finances, which is the entire goal of our app.

In summary, our hypothesis is: The type of graph that shows the user’s main budgeting information will affect the app’s ease of use and understandability.

Independent / Dependent Variables

Describe the factor(s) (independent variable(s)) that you will vary / What dependent (response) variable(s) will you observe?

We will present users with different versions of the app homepage with different graphs (pie graph, bar graph, exploded pie graph, and multiple pie graphs). However, we will keep rest of the section same so we can observe the user’s response to different types of graphs. Our test subjects will be young adults who are familiar with phone applications to minimize any disparity caused by confusion with the device.

Expected Observation

If your hypothesis is correct, what pattern would you expect to observe in the response variables?

If our hypothesis is correct, we expect users to spend different amounts of time on different types of graphs. If users take about similar amounts of time to answer questions regarding the graph, our hypothesis should be rejected and we can have any form of graph on the homepage. If users take different amounts of time to answer questions, we should compare the amount of time that a user required to answer questions and pick the shortest one for the sake of efficiency.

Study Design

Also explain how many subjects you would ideally test, how many iterations each would participate in, and if each participant tries multiple conditions, how these conditions would be ordered and distributed over participants. Note: if your team has no prior experience in the design of controlled experiments, please indicate that in this section.

Since we are doing a quantitative test, we would want to test about 20-40 users to make sure our numbers are statistically significant. Each user would be one of the graphs, so there would be 5-10 users in each of the 4 testing groups. We decided to do this because if one person saw all 4 graphs (or even if each user saw 2 of the graphs), their responses would be affected by having seen the same information before. We considered doing this and just changing the data that is displayed on the graph to avoid affecting the responses, but the user would still recognize elements like icons, color, and words on the screen. This may affect their response times. Therefore, we chose to do only one graph per user.

Script

Imagine you are using this app to view your spending and budgeting. You have used this app for 2 months and you have a checking, savings, and credit card account.

The screen you will view is a graphical representation of your budget. We will ask you to identify certain things and perform certain tasks.

  1. Identifying categories
    1. Where is the food category?
    2. How did you identify it?
  2. Category values
    1. How much have you spent in the food category?
    2. How much do you have left to spend in the food category?
  3. Clicking on categories
    1. How would you open more details about a category?
  4. Ask about icons
    1. What does the first icon mean?
    2. What does the second icon mean?
  5. General Questions
    1. Would overall income/expenses info or categorical info be most useful (or something else)?
    2. Would you like to have graph options for viewing your budget?
