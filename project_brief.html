Project Brief: Team Ladle - "Is life fundamentally miserable?"

Chris Aring, Sarah Barden, Minju Kang, Charlie Owen, Jeremy Ryan

The problem

We would like to address the problem of how young adults struggle with learning life skills for their lives after school.

Our impact

The project will allow users to increase their independence, quality of life, and confidence in learning new life skills. We hope to simultaneously educate about critical issues and add a sense of levity to the process.

Design Ideas

  1. A communication platform for members of a community to talk and ask questions about life skills. Maybe an extension for an existing platform to limit it to friends of friends (of friends?)
  2. Anonymous sharing of real numbers (actual salary, rent, etc)
  3. Reminders for life: Instead of just throwing info at people, remind about different pieces as they go (Inspect your car next month, taxes are due next month, “here is some info to get you started”, doctors appointments, etc.)
  4. Platform for connecting old and young people to teach each other life skills, e.g. young person teaching about technology and an older adult teaching about buying a house
  5. Personal finance management / Tax calculator (How much should I pay for this month’s tax?)
  6. Updating the physical Game of Life to be more relevant to today.

User Interviewing and Assumption Tests

Target Users: any adults (young adults mostly 20-30) who are seeking for life advice and also older adults who have certain skills we want to incorporate into the project. We will interview our perspective users for initial information and later ask them to interact with our prototype. We will use not only the BOW colleges to find interviewees, but also venture out into the surrounding communities to talk to people. We will research what kind of apps/softwares already exist and how well their “user experience” is designed by trying those apps/software ourselves

Possible Barriers

Information, time, legitimacy (what advice to trust), motivation

Goals

We will stick to loops such as design iterations and bi-weekly team assessment, etc. We will also openly accept other people’s opinion about ours and will not hesitate to kill our darling.
